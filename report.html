Reporting FAQ

Common questions about reporting illegal mining activities

What happens after I submit a report?

After submission, your report is reviewed by our team and then forwarded to the appropriate authorities, which may include the local police, forest department, mining department, or pollution control board. If you provided contact information, you may receive updates on the action taken.

Is it safe to report illegal mining?

Your safety is paramount. We recommend reporting anonymously if you have concerns about your safety. Never confront those involved in illegal activities directly. Our system is designed to protect your identity, especially when using the anonymous reporting option.

What evidence is most helpful?

The most valuable evidence includes photos or videos of the illegal mining activity, machinery being used, vehicle license plates, and any identifying information about the operators. Location details with GPS coordinates are extremely helpful. Any documentation of environmental damage is also important.

Can I track the status of my report?

Yes, if you provided contact information or a temporary contact code (for anonymous reports), you can check the status of your report by logging in or using your temporary code. We will also send updates to your provided contact method as the case progresses.

"One Report Made the Difference"

In April 2023, a single report from a local resident in Jharkhand, complete with photos and location data, led to the discovery of a major illegal mining operation. Authorities seized equipment worth ₹3.5 crore and restored the affected forest area. This case demonstrates how one concerned citizen can create significant positive impact.
